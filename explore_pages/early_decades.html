America's Cowboy

Pop culture played a vital role in jeans going main-stream. The Duke himself, John Wayne, pictured here on the set of "The Alamo," remains the quintessential figure of the American cowboy to this day. Of course, no cowpoke is truly equipped without a pair of blue jeans.

Cool Blue

James Dean. The name itself has become eponymous for the unbelievably cool. The motorcycle-riding, womanizing, leather-jacket-sporting star was both the teenage heart throb of the 1950's and the hero of teenage boys. Often clad in blue jeans, Dean, perhaps single-handedly, brought blue jeans into the main stream fashion culture.
